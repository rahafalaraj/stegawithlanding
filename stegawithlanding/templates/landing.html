🔍 What is Steganography?

📚 The Art of Hidden Communication

Steganography is the practice of concealing messages or information within other non-secret text or data. Unlike cryptography, which encrypts data to make it unreadable, steganography hides the very existence of the communication itself.

The word steganography comes from the Greek words "steganos" (meaning hidden or covered) and "graphia" (meaning writing). This ancient art has evolved into sophisticated digital techniques that can hide messages in images, audio files, videos, and even network protocols.

🛡️ Why Use Steganography?

Digital steganography serves multiple purposes: protecting sensitive information, watermarking digital content, covert communication, and security research. By hiding data in plain sight, it provides an additional layer of security beyond traditional encryption methods.

🔬 Steganographic Techniques

🔧

LSB (Least Significant Bit)

The most common technique that replaces the least significant bits of pixel values with message bits. Simple, fast, and virtually undetectable to the human eye. Perfect for beginners and high-capacity hiding.

📊

DCT (Discrete Cosine Transform)

Advanced frequency domain technique that embeds data in the DCT coefficients of images. Highly resistant to compression and image processing. Used in professional applications requiring robustness.

🌐

Spread Spectrum

Military-grade technique that spreads message bits across multiple image locations using cryptographic keys. Provides maximum security and resistance to detection. Ideal for sensitive communications.

🎲

Random LSB

Enhanced LSB technique that uses cryptographic keys to randomize hiding locations. Combines the simplicity of LSB with the security of randomization. Perfect balance of speed and security.

🖼️

Image-in-Image Hiding

Revolutionary technique that hides entire images within other images using advanced bit manipulation. Supports both sequential and randomized hiding patterns for maximum flexibility.

⚔️

XOR Analysis

Forensic technique that reveals exactly where and how data was hidden by comparing original and steganographic images. Essential for security analysis and technique verification.
